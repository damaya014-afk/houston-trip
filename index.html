Metairie → Houston · I-10 West

The Drive

~350 mi, ~5.5 hrs without stops. Leaving 7 AM Friday puts you in Houston ~2–2:30 PM with a Buc-ee's stop — right at check-in time, and still hours of cushion before the Astros' 5:10 gate-open. July 3 holiday traffic can add an hour, so don't linger too long over the Beaver Nuggets.

Both ends are Central time, so no clock change. Fuel up before you leave; the long stretches west of Baton Rouge have sparse options.

The Buc-ee's stop

Louisiana's own I-10 Buc-ee's (Lafayette) isn't open yet, so the reliable one is Baytown, TX — 60,000+ sq ft, ~100 covered pumps, right on I-10 about 30 minutes east of downtown Houston. Perfect as your last fuel + restroom + lunch (and Beaver Nuggets) stop before you arrive.

Watch these I-10 chokepoints

  • Baton Rouge (I-10/I-12 split): chronic congestion — often the worst delay of the trip. Check live nav before you hit it.
  • Atchafalaya Basin Bridge: 18 miles of narrow, shoulderless elevated bridge — one wreck backs it up fast. No exits to bail.
  • Lake Charles (Calcasieu River bridge): ongoing bridge work and bottlenecks.

Open Waze before departure and re-check around Baton Rouge; it'll route you around incidents in real time.

🎟️ Your seats — Section 352, Row M, Seats 6–8

Section 352 is on the 300 (Mezzanine) level, corner behind the north end zone. Two things you'll like: the front rows of these sections hang out over the lower bowl, so the whole section sits closer to the pitch than "300 level" sounds — and you get the best head-on view of the giant videoboard. Row M puts you comfortably mid-section with a clean full-field view; for soccer, corner-end views are great for watching attacks build toward your end.

  • Entry: 352 is on the north side, so the NW or NE entries are your closest — then take the ramp or escalator up to the 300 level. FIFA will signpost gates by ticket; your FIFA app ticket will show your assigned gate — follow that first, ushers can redirect you inside.
  • Seats 6–8: seat 1 sits closest to the lower-numbered neighboring section (351), so you're near that side of the row — handy to know when picking which aisle to enter from.
  • For mom & aunt: elevators serve the upper levels — ask any staff member at entry rather than doing ramps in the heat.

Matchday essentials — tick these off

🧿 Bank of America Fan Band — two ways to get one

The viral beaded bracelet comes in two flavors:

  • At the stadium Saturday (easiest): pick up a pre-made, matchup-themed Fan Band as part of the matchday experience — beads for the two teams playing. No Fan Fest trip required.
  • At the Fan Festival (custom): build your own from 140 beads + a photo booth. Demand went viral, so it now runs on a timed-wristband system — line up before gates open, get a paper wristband for a one-hour slot, then visit the BofA station in your window.

Bottom line: if you just want the keepsake, grab it Saturday at the match and keep Sunday for Space Center. Only make the Sunday Fan Fest trip if you want the customizable one.

Beating the lines — bracelet + scarf + Fan ID

You can do all of it if you get ahead of the crowd. The matchday festival and sponsor activations open ~3 hours before kickoff (so from ~9 AM outside the stadium), while gates open ~90 min prior. Plan:

  • Arrive early — be at the stadium plaza by ~10 AM, not 10:30. The BofA Fan Band is the viral, long-line item, so hit it first.
  • Split up. Three of you = your biggest advantage. One holds the Fan Band line while the others grab the scarf (merch stand) and the free FIFA Fan ID, then swap. You'll cover everything in a fraction of the time.
  • Grab your free FIFA Fan ID — a keepsake card for ticket holders, separate from the bracelet. Collect it at your first match.
  • Scarf (paid, ~$40 at merch stands): confirmed not a giveaway — so zero urgency. Buy it whenever the line is short: mid–first half or halftime are the dead zones. Merch is cashless; card or phone pay only.

Doing this means being on the Red Line closer to 9:45 AM than 10:15 — worth it to skip the worst of the bracelet line. The scarf can wait; the bracelet can't.

Optional Sunday — FIFA Fan Festival (free, no ticket)

Worth a Sunday morning only if you want the custom Fan Band or just love the atmosphere: every match on giant screens, ~20 food vendors, a playable pitch, an air-conditioned 360° dome (Esphera), plus misting, shade and water.

2301 Dallas St, EaDo, by Shell Energy Stadium. Green or Purple Line → EaDo/Stadium Station, ~5-min walk. For the custom bracelet, arrive before doors open to grab a timed wristband at the entry gate.

Heat caution: there have already been heat-related hospitalizations at this Fan Fest this summer. Beeline to the AC dome/pavilions, hydrate constantly, and consider the ~$95 VIP indoor option if you want guaranteed AC and seating for your mom and aunt.

Scheduling note: Space Center is your Sunday plan. If you do both, hit the Fan Fest early for the bracelet, then drive south to Space Center for the afternoon (open till 6).

🎆 July 4th fireworks — after the Fan Fest closes (6 PM)

Houston's big show is Freedom Over Texas (39th year, America's 250th) at Eleanor Tinsley & Sam Houston Parks on Buffalo Bayou, just west of downtown. Festival runs 3–10 PM; the fireworks finale is ~9:30 PM. My picks for your group, easiest first:

  • Free viewing along Buffalo Bayou / Allen Parkway / Memorial Dr — the show is choreographed in the sky west of downtown, viewable for free from the bayou trail bridges, the park hillside, and elevated downtown parking decks. A short walk or quick ride from your hotel; skip the $10–15 ticket and the crowd.
  • POST Houston Skylawn — a 5-acre downtown rooftop park with skyline + fireworks views (free, but reserve a spot in advance). Elevated and breezier than ground level.
  • Miller Outdoor Theatre (Hermann Park) — the most relaxed option: free Houston Symphony "Star-Spangled Salute" at 8:30 PM with a fireworks finale ~10 PM, hillside seating, on the Red Line. Lovely and lower-stress for your mom and aunt. Download the Pyrocast app for synced audio.

Heat + crowd note: it's a Saturday, so traffic and parking fill early — arrive 60–90 min ahead, hydrate, and lean on the Red Line over driving.

Quick corrections to your notes

Bag the jersey AND the First Bud

  • Grab the jersey on entry, before leaving the distribution area.
  • Then the Michelob Ultra Club — confirmed location: Upper Deck, Right Field corner (300 level), a 360° public bar with skyline views (staircase up from the Silverado Mezzanine; Killen's BBQ is right there). First Bud Friday runs 5–7 PM, $5 Budweiser drafts + live DJ.

From your seats — Section 236

You're on the Honda Club Level (200 tier), right-field / first-base corner — the same side as the Michelob Ultra Club, so the pregame is an easy hop. The play:

  • Don't go to 236 first. At gate-open (~5:10), grab your jersey at entry, then head straight along the right-field side and up to the upper-deck right-field corner where the Ultra Club sits (staircase up from the mezzanine — any usher will point you). First Bud runs 5–7, so beat the cutoff.
  • Then drop down to Section 236 for first pitch — you're already on the right side of the park, so it's a short move down a level.
  • Check your ticket for a $15 voucher — sections 233–236 sometimes include food/drink/merch credit. If yours has it, it covers the First Bud and then some.

Heads up: the right-field corner is the sunny side, but for a 7:10 July game the roof is almost certainly closed and the A/C on, so it won't matter.

Getting Around

The rail cheat-sheet (METRORail)

  • Red Line runs through downtown (Main Street Square, steps from your hotel) straight to Stadium Park/Astrodome by NRG — your World Cup line. ~Every 5 min with two-car trains during the Cup.
  • Green or Purple Line → EaDo/Stadium Station for the Fan Festival.
  • Fares: ~$1.25/ride, ~$3 day pass via the METRO Q app, a Q Card, or tap. Grab the discounted senior fare for your mom and aunt.

Driving & parking

  • Hotel parking is paid (downtown valet ~$30–45/night) — confirm at check-in.
  • Don't drive to NRG on match day — roads are restricted and lots need pre-purchase. Take the Red Line.
  • Daikin Park is walkable (~0.5 mi) — Friday you likely don't need car or rail.
  • Space Center is the exception — drive or rideshare; no rail link.

Rideshare & wayfinding

Uber/Lyft work everywhere with dedicated lots at NRG and the Fan Fest. Expect surge after the noon match and on July 4th evening — rail dodges it. Downtown, follow the blue hexagon ground markings and purple "Via Fútbol" signs to the stadium, Fan Fest and rail.

Quick answers

FAQ & "Did you think of…"

What am I not considering?

The jersey is one-size XL (souvenir, not wearable for everyone); a noon kickoff means an early Saturday start (be on the Red Line ~10:15); July 4th road closures + rideshare surge after the match and that evening; valet + daily parking is a real cost even though you'll mostly ride rail; you arrive ~1 PM Friday but check-in is ~3 PM (ask about bag storage / early check-in); the stadium is cashless with a clear-bag policy and tickets must live in the FIFA app wallet; and pace the days around the heat for your mom and aunt.

Should I get travel insurance / refundable rates?

Given ticket uncertainty, book hotel and add-ons at refundable rates where possible, and keep World Cup plans flexible until tickets are in your FIFA wallet. Not financial advice — just hedging variables you don't control.

Is the match guaranteed to be at noon?

Scheduled for 12:00 PM CT. FIFA notes kickoff times can shift; confirm on FIFA.com the week of. Teams finalize as the bracket resolves.

Where exactly is the Michelob Ultra Club?

Confirmed: Upper Deck, Right Field corner (300 level) — a 360° public bar with skyline views, up the staircase from the Silverado Mezzanine, next to Killen's BBQ. First Bud Friday there runs 5–7 PM.

Can we do the match and the Fan Fest the same day?

Tight but possible — different rail lines (Red for NRG, Green/Purple for EaDo), so allow a downtown transfer at Main Street Square (~30–40 min). Match ends ~2 PM; Fan Fest closes 6 PM on the 4th.
